# SKILL: Objection Crusher — Mortgage Edition
# Step 7 of 9 — Pre-Handle Resistance

## WHEN TO USE
After the sales letter structure is drafted.
Insert objection handling into the sales letter lead, method, or proof sections
— or use as standalone content for email follow-ups and call scripts.

The goal: by the time they get to the CTA, every reason NOT to act has been addressed.

---

## THE 8 CORE MORTGAGE OBJECTIONS

---

### OBJECTION 1 — "My credit isn't good enough."

**What they're really saying:**
"I'm afraid to find out the answer. I'd rather not know than be told no again."

**The truth:**
FHA accepts 580+. Some portfolio lenders go lower.
More importantly, "good enough" depends on which lender and which product.
One lender's decline is another's approval.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"The question isn't whether your credit is perfect — it's whether you've talked to the right lender.
FHA was designed for credit scores that don't qualify conventional.
I've helped buyers with 580 credit scores get into homes. The number matters less than you think."

**In sales letter placement:** Villain section or Method Step 1.

---

### OBJECTION 2 — "I don't have enough for a down payment."

**What they're really saying:**
"I believe I need 20% and I don't have it."

**The truth:**
FHA: 3.5% down.
VA: $0 down.
DPA programs in MD and DC can cover the down payment entirely.
Conventional: as low as 5%.
The 20% myth has stopped more buyers than any other single piece of misinformation.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"You've probably heard you need 20% down. That's been false for decades.
FHA requires 3.5%. VA requires nothing.
Maryland and DC have programs that will cover your down payment entirely — grants you don't pay back.
The real question is: how much do you have? Let's work from there."

**In sales letter placement:** Lead section or after headline.

---

### OBJECTION 3 — "I've been declined before."

**What they're really saying:**
"I tried and failed. I don't want to be humiliated again."

**The truth:**
A decline from one retail bank is not a universal verdict.
Different lenders have different guidelines. Wholesale lenders have more flexibility.
The reason for the decline often has a fixable path.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"One bank's no is not the final answer.
Banks work with one set of underwriting criteria. If you don't fit their mold exactly, you're out.
I work with multiple wholesale lenders — each with different criteria and different flexibility.
If you've been declined, I'd like to know why. Because that's usually a problem I can work around."

**In sales letter placement:** Villain section or intro of Method.

---

### OBJECTION 4 — "Interest rates are too high right now."

**What they're really saying:**
"I'm waiting for a better time." (Which often means never.)

**The truth:**
You date the rate, you marry the house.
When rates drop, you refinance. When prices rise because "everyone waited," you can't buy back in.
The math on renting vs. buying is often in favor of buying even at today's rates,
especially with DPA covering the down payment.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"Rates today are not the rate you'll have forever.
When rates drop — and they will — you refinance.
But if prices go up while you wait, you've lost twice: you paid more rent AND more house.
The real question is whether you can afford the monthly payment on what you want to buy today.
Let's run those numbers before you decide to wait."

**In sales letter placement:** After Method steps or in email follow-up sequences.

---

### OBJECTION 5 — "I'll just go with my bank."

**What they're really saying:**
"I trust familiarity. I don't know the difference between you and Chase."

**The truth:**
A retail bank offers their products at retail prices.
Kenneth offers wholesale access — same money, different channel, often better pricing and more options.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"Your bank will offer you their products. That's it.
I work with the same money markets your bank uses — but at the wholesale level.
More options. Different underwriting criteria. Often better pricing.
It's the difference between going to one store and going to a buyer's agent who shops all of them."

**In sales letter placement:** Mechanism section or FAQ below CTA.

---

### OBJECTION 6 — "I'm not ready yet. Maybe in a few months."

**What they're really saying:**
"I'm afraid of commitment. I don't want to start something I can't finish."

**The truth:**
Talking to Kenneth costs nothing and commits to nothing.
A free 15-minute call gives them information they can use whenever they ARE ready.
The call is not the purchase.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"You don't have to be ready to buy in order to have this conversation.
A 15-minute call costs nothing, doesn't touch your credit, and gives you a clear picture of where you stand.
If you decide you're not ready after the call — you're better informed than you were before.
That's the only risk."

**In sales letter placement:** CTA section / directly above the booking button.

---

### OBJECTION 7 — "Can I trust a mortgage broker?" (Realtor version: "Can I trust this LO?")

**What they're really saying:**
"I've seen lenders make promises and disappear. I don't want to put my client in bad hands."

**The truth:**
Kenneth's Zillow rating is public and verified.
10+ years = he's been in the market through multiple cycles.
The mechanism (same-day pre-approval, 10-day close) is specific and testable — not a vague claim.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"I understand the skepticism. You've worked with lenders who said the right things and then dropped the ball at closing.
Here's what I offer instead: specifics.
Same-day pre-approval — not 'fast,' same day.
10 business day close — not 'quick,' ten days.
5 stars on Zillow — not 'highly rated,' you can read every review.
I'm not asking you to trust me. I'm asking you to test me on one transaction."

**In sales letter placement:** Proof section or realtor-specific email outreach.

---

### OBJECTION 8 — "This is too complicated / I don't understand the process."

**What they're really saying:**
"I'm overwhelmed. I don't want to look stupid."

**The truth:**
Kenneth explains everything. No jargon. No assumptions.
The 15-minute call is specifically designed to demystify — not to sell.

**How to crush it in copy:**
"I've been doing this for 10 years. I've explained the process to every type of person imaginable.
My job is to make it simple — not to impress you with terminology.
On our first call, I'll tell you in plain language exactly what you qualify for, what you'd pay,
and what the process looks like step by step. No jargon. No pressure."

**In sales letter placement:** Method steps or CTA close.

## HOW TO DEPLOY OBJECTION RESPONSES

| Format | Where objections go |
|---|---|
| Sales letter | 2–3 objections woven into lead, villain, and method sections |
| Facebook ad copy | 1 objection crushed in the body copy (the most relevant one for that angle) |
| Email follow-up (day 2) | Subject: "The reason most people don't do this..." — pick Objection 4 or 6 |
| Call script | Objection handlers at the end of `07_objection-crusher` as live talking points |
| SMS follow-up | 1-sentence version only ("You don't need 20% down — DM me and I'll show you") |
