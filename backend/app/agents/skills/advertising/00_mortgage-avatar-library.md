# Step 0 of 9 — Foundation (loaded first, referenced throughout entire chain)

## WHEN TO USE
Load this file at the start of every ad campaign build.
It defines the exact person being targeted — their situation, their internal language,
their fears, and what they are silently saying to themselves.

Do not generalize. Do not write for "first-time homebuyers" as a demographic.
Write for the specific person described below.

## THE FOUR MD/DC MORTGAGE AVATARS

---

### AVATAR 1 — THE DECLINED BUYER

**Who they are:**
Marcus or Keisha. 32–44. Working professional. Has been trying to buy a home for 6–18 months.
Got pre-approval somewhere, then got denied at underwriting or denied outright.
Has decent income ($60k–$110k household). Has some savings. Doesn't fully understand why they were declined.
Lives in Prince George's County, Montgomery County, or DC proper.

**Current situation:**
Renting at $1,800–$2,400/month. Watching their rent go up every year.
Feels like the system is working against them. Has talked to one or two lenders — maybe a big bank or an online lender — and got a "no" or a confusing non-answer.

**Their internal language (exact words they say to themselves):**
- "I've been working hard for years. I should be able to buy a house."
- "I don't understand why they keep saying no."
- "Maybe I'm just not ready. Maybe I'll never be ready."
- "The bank doesn't care about my situation — they just look at a number."
- "I have money. I just don't fit their mold."
- "Is there someone who will actually fight for me?"

**Pain points:**
- Feels embarrassed about the decline — hasn't told family
- Frustrated at the lack of explanation from lenders
- Scared to apply again and get another hit on their credit
- Doesn't know what DPA programs exist that could solve their down payment gap
- Feels like banks only work for people who already have money

**Biggest fear:**
Still renting in 5 years, watching home prices go up, never getting in.

**What they've already tried:**
Bank of America, Wells Fargo, or Rocket Mortgage online form. Maybe one local credit union.
All gave generic answers or just said "your debt-to-income is too high."

**What they believe:**
Banks are rigid. They only approve "perfect" people.
They don't know brokers/wholesale LOs exist with more options.
They think the answer is to "wait until things improve."

**Schwartz awareness stage:** STAGE 2 — Problem Aware
They know they have a problem. They do not know Kenneth or his specific solution exists.
Lead the ad with their problem — not your product.

**Hook formula that works:**
"If banks keep declining you [even though X]..." → validate → reveal the real reason → introduce new mechanism

### AVATAR 2 — THE ANXIOUS FIRST-TIMER

**Who they are:**
Taylor or DeShawn. 26–36. First job with real income or recently married.
Never bought a home. Parents rented. No one in their immediate circle to ask.
Income: $45k–$80k. Credit: fair to good (620–700). Savings: $3k–$12k.
Maryland suburbs — Laurel, Hyattsville, Silver Spring, Waldorf.

**Current situation:**
Renting with a partner or roommates. Knows they "should" be buying.
Has googled mortgage stuff but comes away more confused than when they started.
Afraid to talk to a lender because they think the lender will judge them or pressure them.

**Their internal language:**
- "I don't even know where to start."
- "What if I do something wrong and mess it up?"
- "I don't have enough saved for a down payment... right?"
- "I don't want to waste someone's time if I'm not ready."
- "What if my credit isn't good enough?"
- "Everyone else seems to have figured this out. Why can't I?"

**Pain points:**
- Information overload — every article contradicts another
- Feels like they'll be judged for not knowing the basics
- Doesn't know about FHA (3.5% down) or DPA programs
- Thinks they need 20% down (this is the #1 myth)
- Afraid to find out their credit score because "it might be bad"

**Biggest fear:**
Making a huge financial mistake that haunts them for 30 years.
Also: being judged for not knowing what they're doing.

**What they've already tried:**
Reddit threads. YouTube videos. Maybe used a mortgage calculator online.
Has not talked to a real lender yet.

**What they believe:**
Homebuying is complicated and only for people who "have it together."
You need 20% down or you can't buy.
The process takes months and is full of traps.

**Schwartz awareness stage:** STAGE 1-2 — Problem Aware, Borderline Unaware
They feel discomfort (renting, not building equity) but haven't fully connected it to action.
Lead the ad with their dream or with the myth-busting fact ("You don't need 20% down").
Do NOT open with your product. Open with their feeling.

**Hook formula that works:**
"Nobody tells first-time buyers this, but..." → myth bust → make it feel simple → soft CTA

### AVATAR 3 — THE EQUITY PRISONER

**Who they are:**
Diane or Robert. 42–58. Has owned their home for 5–15 years.
Built significant equity ($80k–$250k) but feels "stuck."
High-interest debt (credit cards, car loans). Monthly payments are eating their paycheck.
Property in MD suburbs — Bowie, Rockville, Columbia, Gaithersburg.

**Current situation:**
Knows they have equity but doesn't know how to access it responsibly.
Has thought about a HELOC or cash-out refi but is confused by the process.
May have gotten a mailer from a random lender but didn't trust it.
Monthly debt payments are $2,500–$4,000. Feeling squeezed.

**Their internal language:**
- "I have all this equity but I can't touch it."
- "My monthly payments are killing me."
- "I wish I could just consolidate everything into one payment."
- "I don't want to lose my house trying to fix my debt."
- "Is this even smart? What if rates go higher?"
- "I've worked too hard to mess this up."

**Pain points:**
- Doesn't understand the difference between HELOC, cash-out refi, and second lien
- Fears losing their home if they take on more debt
- Doesn't know their current equity amount
- Suspicious of lenders trying to talk them into something
- Wants to do the math before committing to anything

**Biggest fear:**
Making a move that puts their home at risk. Regret.

**What they've already tried:**
Maybe called their current bank or servicer. Got a confusing answer.
May have used an online equity calculator.

**What they believe:**
Their equity is "locked up." Accessing it is risky.
The process is confusing and they need someone to explain it simply, not sell them something.

**Schwartz awareness stage:** STAGE 2-3 — Solution Aware
They know they have a problem AND they know solutions exist (HELOC/refi).
They don't know Kenneth. Lead with clarity and simplicity, not urgency.

**Hook formula that works:**
"You have $[X] in equity sitting in your home right now. Here's how to use it without risking the house."
→ explain the options simply → show the math → CTA to run numbers

### AVATAR 4 — THE REALTOR'S CLIENT

**Who they are:**
Jasmine or Anthony. 29–42. Has a realtor they trust. Ready to make an offer.
Found their home or is actively touring. Needs a pre-approval letter — fast.
Income: $65k–$130k. Credit: 680–750. Has cash ready.
May be competing against other offers. Urgency is real.

**Current situation:**
Their realtor told them they need a pre-approval from a reputable lender.
They may already have one from a big bank but it's taking too long or the rates are off.
They're in "close mode" — the dream is in front of them, they just need the financing to not slow them down.

**Their internal language:**
- "I found the house. I don't want to lose it because of the mortgage."
- "I need this lender to move fast."
- "My realtor recommended someone. I hope they're good."
- "I just need the letter today. Can they do that?"
- "I don't want to be embarrassed in front of my realtor."

**Pain points:**
- Time — every day they wait, another offer could come in
- Fear their lender will drop the ball at closing
- Worried about rate fluctuation between now and close
- Doesn't want to restart the process with a new lender

**Biggest fear:**
Losing the home because financing fell through or moved too slow.

**Schwartz awareness stage:** STAGE 4 — Product Aware
They know they need a lender. They are actively comparing.
Lead with PROOF and SPEED. Same-day pre-approval. 10-day close track record. Reliability.

**Hook formula that works:**
Proof-first: "Same-day pre-approval. 10 business day close. Your clients won't lose a home waiting on me."
→ Fast, direct, credibility-focused → Realtor sends the referral

## HOW TO USE THIS FILE IN THE CHAIN

When running any step of the campaign chain:
1. Identify which avatar is being targeted
2. Pull their "internal language" verbatim into ad copy — these are the words that trigger recognition
3. Never generalize — a declined buyer and a first-timer have completely different fears
4. The Schwartz stage determines WHERE the copy starts (problem, solution, or product)
5. The hook formula is the opening line of every ad and the headline of every sales letter
