# SKILL: Schwartz Awareness Mapper — Mortgage Edition
# Step 2 of 9 — Awareness Diagnosis

## WHEN TO USE
After avatar is identified and offer is extracted.
Before writing a single word of copy.

This skill determines WHERE to enter the conversation in the prospect's mind.
Wrong stage = dead ad. Right stage = they stop scrolling and read everything.

---

## THE FIVE STAGES (Mortgage-Translated)

### STAGE 1 — UNAWARE
**They don't know they have a problem.**
Living paycheck to paycheck, renting, not thinking about homeownership.
Has never searched "how to buy a house."

**Copy approach:** Start with the dream or a provocative question.
Open with lifestyle (building wealth, leaving something for your kids, stopping rent increases).
Do NOT mention mortgages, pre-approvals, or lenders.

**Example opener:**
"At $2,100/month in rent, you've paid $25,200 this year. How much of that do you own? Zero."

**Use for:** Cold Facebook/Instagram audiences who fit the demographic but haven't engaged.

---

### STAGE 2 — PROBLEM AWARE
**They know they have a problem. They don't know the solution.**
The declined buyer who knows they keep getting rejected.
The first-timer who knows they "should" be buying but doesn't know how.
The equity prisoner who knows their money is stuck.

**Copy approach:** Agitate the problem. Validate their frustration. Then introduce that a solution exists.
Do NOT pitch your product yet. Pitch the existence of a better way.

**Example opener (Declined Buyer):**
"If banks keep declining your mortgage — even though you have income, savings, and a job — it's not you. It's their criteria. There's a different path."

**Example opener (First-Timer):**
"Nobody tells first-time buyers this: you don't need 20% down. You never did."

**Use for:** Retargeting audiences who've visited your site. Warm Facebook audiences. People who've engaged with content but haven't booked.

---

### STAGE 3 — SOLUTION AWARE
**They know solutions exist. They're comparing options.**
The equity prisoner who knows HELOCs exist but hasn't committed.
The buyer who's gotten pre-qualified somewhere but isn't moving forward.
The realtor's client who's shopping lenders.

**Copy approach:** Introduce YOUR mechanism as superior to the alternatives.
They know what a HELOC is — now show them why yours is better (wholesale rates, fast turnaround, honest math).
Start to differentiate Kenneth specifically.

**Example opener:**
"Most HELOCs take 45 days to close. I've done them in under 3 weeks. And I'll show you the math before you commit."

**Use for:** Engaged warm audiences. People who've used calculators on your site. Email list.

---

### STAGE 4 — PRODUCT AWARE
**They know Kenneth exists (or know about the type of service he offers).**
Realtor referrals — their agent mentioned Kenneth by name.
Past leads who've seen content but haven't booked.
Website visitors who hit the rates page or the booking page but didn't convert.

**Copy approach:** Remove objections. Show proof. Make the CTA feel safe and low-commitment.
Lead with Zillow rating, closing speed, specific results.
Make booking a call feel like the obvious next step, not a commitment.

**Example opener:**
"Same-day pre-approval. Close in 10 business days. 5-star Zillow. Just book the call — 15 minutes, no pressure, real numbers."

**Use for:** Retargeting ads. Email to warm list. Realtor outreach.

---

### STAGE 5 — MOST AWARE
**They're ready to move. They just need to be asked.**
Someone who's gone through intake form but hasn't booked.
Someone who texted/emailed but got busy.
Someone the agent personally spoke to once.

**Copy approach:** Direct, simple, low-friction. Remove every possible barrier.
Just the CTA. Maybe a reminder of the key benefit.

**Example opener:**
"Still thinking about it? Here's the 15-minute call link. No paperwork, no credit pull — just real numbers."

**Use for:** SMS follow-up. Email re-engagement. Personal DM from Kenneth.

---

## DIAGNOSIS CHECKLIST

Before writing copy, answer these three questions:

1. **Does this person know they have a problem?**
   - Yes → Stage 2+
   - No → Stage 1

2. **Do they know a solution like Kenneth's exists?**
   - Yes → Stage 3+
   - No → Stage 2

3. **Do they know Kenneth specifically?**
   - Yes → Stage 4+
   - No → Stages 1–3

## AVATAR → STAGE QUICK REFERENCE

| Avatar | Default Stage | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Declined Buyer | Stage 2 | Knows the problem. Doesn't know wholesale LOs exist. |
| Anxious First-Timer | Stage 1–2 | Vaguely uncomfortable. Doesn't know what they don't know. |
| Equity Prisoner | Stage 2–3 | Knows equity is stuck. May know HELOCs exist but not Kenneth. |
| Realtor's Client | Stage 4 | Has a realtor. Actively comparing lenders. |

---

## CRITICAL RULES

- Never start at a stage ABOVE where the prospect actually is.
  Starting at Stage 4 with a Stage 2 audience = they ignore you because they don't know why they should care.

- Never pitch your product to a Stage 1 audience.
  They're not ready to hear it. Pitch the problem or the dream first.

- The awareness stage determines the FIRST SENTENCE of every ad and sales letter.
  Everything after that can follow the offer structure — but that first sentence is determined by this map.
